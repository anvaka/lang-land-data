# **劳驾 (láo jià) - Excuse me / Would you please... (polite request)**

**Character Breakdown**:  
- **劳 (láo)**: means "toil" or "labor." Left: ⺍ (small, bottom), right: 力 (lì) "strength."  
- **驾 (jià)**: means "to drive (a vehicle)" or "to command." Top: 加 (jiā), bottom: 马 (mǎ, horse).

**Examples**:  
- 劳驾，请让一让。(Láo jià, qǐng ràng yí ràng.) - Excuse me, please let me pass.  
- 劳驾，这个座位有人吗？(Láo jià, zhè ge zuòwèi yǒu rén ma?) - Excuse me, is this seat taken?  
- 劳驾，厕所在哪儿？(Láo jià, cèsuǒ zài nǎr?) - Excuse me, where is the restroom?

**Usage Notes**:  
"劳驾 (láo jià)" is a super-polite way to get someone's attention or to ask for help (e.g., in public or with strangers). Similar to "Excuse me" in English, but a bit more formal.

**Memory Aids**:  
Imagine you're asking a driver (驾, jià) working hard (劳, láo) to do you a favor-“Excuse me, could you (labor to) drive for me?”
