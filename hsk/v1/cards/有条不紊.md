# **有条不紊 (yǒu tiáo bù wěn) - Organized; in good order**

**Character Breakdown**:  
- 有 (yǒu): to have; contains radical 月 (yuè, "moon") on the left and 𠂇 (right hand) on the right  
- 条 (tiáo): strip, item; bamboo radical ⺮ (zhú, top), character 夂 (zhǐ, bottom)  
- 不 (bù): not; horizontal 一 (yī, "one", top) and Hook 丿 (piě, slashing stroke underneath)  
- 紊 (wěn): disorder; silk radical 纟 (sī, left), 文 (wén, right, "literature" or "culture")

**Examples**:  
- 她(tā) 做事(zuò shì) 总是(zǒng shì) 有条不紊(yǒu tiáo bù wěn)。 (Tā zuò shì zǒng shì yǒu tiáo bù wěn.) - She always does things in an orderly way.  
- 老师(lǎo shī) 有条不紊(yǒu tiáo bù wěn) 地(dì) 组织(zǔ zhī) 课堂(kè táng)。 (Lǎo shī yǒu tiáo bù wěn de zǔ zhī kè táng.) - The teacher organizes the class in an orderly fashion.  
- 会议(huì yì) 进行(jìn xíng) 得(de) 有条不紊(yǒu tiáo bù wěn)。 (Huì yì jìn xíng de yǒu tiáo bù wěn.) - The meeting is proceeding in good order.

**Usage Notes**:  
- 有条不紊(yǒu tiáo bù wěn) is commonly used as an adjective/adverbial phrase.  
- Often describes work, processes, or events that are well-organized and systematic.

**Memory Aids**:  
- Imagine every "strip" (条[tiáo]) is "not" (不[bù]) in "disorder" (紊[wěn]), so everything "has" (有[yǒu]) order.  
- Visualize tidy files lined up (条[tiáo]), never messy (不紊[bù wěn]).
