# **茶 (chá) - tea**

**Character Breakdown**:  
- 茶 (chá): Top: 艹 (cǎo) radical - means "grass" or "plant", hints the word is plant-related.
  Middle: 人 (rén) component - resembles a person or spreading branches.
  Bottom: 木 (mù) - means "tree" or "wood", relates to plants/trees.
  The character’s structure (top: 艹, middle: 人, bottom: 木) visually combines elements for plants and trees.

**Examples**:  
- 我喜欢喝茶。(Wǒ xǐhuān hē chá.) - I like drinking tea.  
- 你想要什么茶？(Nǐ xiǎng yào shénme chá?) - What kind of tea do you want?  
- 下午我们一起喝茶吧。(Xiàwǔ wǒmen yìqǐ hē chá ba.) - Let’s have tea together this afternoon.

**Usage Notes**:  
- 茶 (chá) is both a general term for "tea" and, with modifiers, describes specific kinds (e.g., 绿茶 (lǜchá): green tea, 红茶 (hóngchá): black tea).  
- Commonly used in daily life, cafes, and social invitations.

**Memory Aids**:  
Imagine a person (人 (rén)) sitting under a tree (木 (mù)), picking grass/leaves (艹 (cǎo))-just like harvesting tea leaves!
