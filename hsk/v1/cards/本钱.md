# **本钱 (běn qián) - Capital; Principal (investment)**

**Character Breakdown**:  
- **本 (běn)**: "root" or "origin". Top: "一 (yī)" (one), Bottom: "木 (mù)" (tree), suggesting the "origin" or "foundation".  
- **钱 (qián)**: "money". Left: "钅(jīn)" (metal/gold radical), Right: "戋 (jiān)" (narrow/slight), the whole suggests a coin or money.

**Examples**:  
- 我(wǒ)没有(méi yǒu)足够(zú gòu)的(de)本钱(běn qián)。 (Wǒ méiyǒu zúgòu de běnqián.) - I don't have enough capital.  
- 他(tā)用(yòng)所有(suǒ yǒu)的(de)本钱(běn qián)开(kāi)了(le)公司(gōng sī)。 (Tā yòng suǒyǒu de běnqián kāile gōngsī.) - He used all his capital to start a company.  
- 你(nǐ)要(yào)学会(xué huì)如何(rú hé)保住(bǎo zhù)本钱(běn qián)。 (Nǐ yào xuéhuì rúhé bǎozhù běnqián.) - You need to learn how to preserve your capital.

**Usage Notes**:  
- 本钱 (běn qián) refers to money invested, often in business. It can also mean figurative "resources" or "assets" enabling someone to do something.

**Memory Aids**:  
- 本 (běn) is the “root”-the *main* source; 钱 (qián) is “money.”  
- Think: “Root money = principal (investment capital).”
