# **麦克风 (màikèfēng) - Microphone**

**Character Breakdown**:  
- **麦 (mài)**: wheat; top radical “⺾” (cǎo, grass) over “来” (lái, come)  
- **克 (kè)**: overcome; top “儿” (ér, son) over inside “古” (gǔ, ancient)  
- **风 (fēng)**: wind; left-side “几” (jī, table) with right curve, bottom “丶” (zhǔ, dot)

**Examples**:  
- 请(pǐng)把(bǎ)麦克风(màikèfēng)递给(dìgěi)我(wǒ)。 (Qǐng bǎ màikèfēng dì gěi wǒ.) - Please hand me the microphone.  
- 她(tā)用(yòng)麦克风(màikèfēng)唱歌(chànggē)。 (Tā yòng màikèfēng chànggē.) - She sings with a microphone.  
- 这个(zhège)麦克风(màikèfēng)有(yǒu)杂音(záyīn)。 (Zhège màikèfēng yǒu záyīn.) - This microphone has noise.

**Usage Notes**:  
- 麦克风 (màikèfēng) is a loanword from “microphone.”  
- Common in both formal and casual settings.

**Memory Aids**:  
- Think “麦(mài)克(kè)” = “Mike” (as in the name) and “风(fēng)” = “phone” (sounds like “fone”); Mike speaks into the wind!
