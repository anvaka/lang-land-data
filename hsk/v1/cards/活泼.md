# **活泼 (huó pō) - lively; active**

**Character Breakdown**:  
- **活 (huó)**: “Water” radical (氵, left), “tongue” (舌, right). Together suggest “alive” or “living.”  
- **泼 (pō)**: “Water” radical (氵, left), “split/throw” phonetic (发/皿, right). Indicates “splash,” by extension “energetic.”

**Examples**:  
- 她(tā)是(shì)个(gè)活泼(huó pō)的(de)孩子(hái zi)。 (Tā shì gè huópō de háizi.) - She is a lively child.  
- 那(nà)只(zhī)狗(gǒu)很(hěn)活泼(huó pō)。 (Nà zhī gǒu hěn huópō.) - That dog is very energetic.  
- 他(tā)在(zài)班(bān)里(lǐ)总(zǒng)是(shì)最(zuì)活泼(huó pō)的(de)一(yī)个(gè)。 (Tā zài bān lǐ zǒng shì zuì huópō de yī gè.) - He is always the most lively one in class.

**Usage Notes**:  
- Used for people, animals, sometimes places or atmosphere.  
- Positive connotation, often describes children’s personalities.

**Memory Aids**:  
- 活(huó): “Water” + “tongue” = someone whose tongue is moving, full of life!  
- 泼(pō): Imagine lively kids “splashing” (泼, pō) water everywhere.
