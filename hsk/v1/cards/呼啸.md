# **呼啸 (hūxiào) - to whistle; to scream; to howl**

**Character Breakdown**:  
- **呼 (hū)**: mouth radical (口 kǒu) on the left + "status" (乎 hū) on the right; means "to call" or "to exhale loudly".  
- **啸 (xiào)**: mouth radical (口 kǒu) on the left + "emerging" (肃 sù without upper dots) on the right; means "to whistle" or "to hiss".

**Examples**:  
- 风呼啸着穿过树林。(Fēng hūxiào zhe chuānguò shùlín.) - The wind howled through the forest.  
- 火车呼啸而过。(Huǒchē hūxiào ér guò.) - The train whistled as it passed by.  
- 孩子们呼啸着奔跑。(Háizimen hūxiào zhe bēnpǎo.) - The children ran while screaming.

**Usage Notes**:  
呼啸 (hūxiào) describes loud, continuous sounds (wind, machines, or voices). Often used with verbs to show ongoing action (e.g., 呼啸着 (hūxiào zhe)).

**Memory Aids**:  
Imagine a "mouth" (口 kǒu) on the left making a loud sound, while "whistling" (啸 xiào) brings out a sharp, high-pitched tone-呼啸 (hūxiào) is a forceful, whistling call.
