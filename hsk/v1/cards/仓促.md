# **仓促 (cāng cù) - Hasty; in a rush**

**Character Breakdown**:  
- **仓** (cāng): "Storehouse." Radical: 广 (guǎng, "shelter") on top, with 仓 (cāng) below. Suggests a building or place.  
- **促** (cù): "To urge; hurried." Left: 亻 (rén, "person" radical). Right: 足 (zú, "foot"), symbolizing moving quickly.

**Examples**:  
- 他(tā) 做(zuò) 作业(zuòyè) 很(hěn) 仓促(cāngcù)。 (Tā zuò zuòyè hěn cāngcù.) - He did his homework hastily.  
- 仓促(cāngcù) 决定(juédìng) 往往(wǎngwǎng) 不好(bù hǎo)。 (Cāngcù juédìng wǎngwǎng bù hǎo.) - Hasty decisions are often bad.  
- 我们(wǒmen) 仓促(cāngcù) 离开(líkāi) 了(le)。 (Wǒmen cāngcù líkāi le.) - We left in a hurry.

**Usage Notes**:  
- **仓促** (cāngcù) is usually an adverb describing doing things quickly and without preparation. Can modify verbs or actions.

**Memory Aids**:  
- Imagine a **仓** (cāng, warehouse) being packed up **促** (cù, quickly: "person's feet")-a person rushing to empty a warehouse, feeling hasty.
