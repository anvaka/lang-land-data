# **短信 (duǎn xìn) - text message / SMS**

**Character Breakdown**:  
- **短** (duǎn): "short." Left: "矢" (shǐ, arrow); right: "豆" (dòu, bean). Suggests something brief or not long.  
- **信** (xìn): "message, letter, trust." Left: "亻" (rén, person radical) on the left; right: "言" (yán, speech) on the right. Implies a person communicating.

**Examples**:  
- 我收到了你的短信。(Wǒ shōu dào le nǐ de duǎn xìn.) - I received your text message.  
- 请给我发短信。(Qǐng gěi wǒ fā duǎn xìn.) - Please send me a text message.  
- 他常常用短信联系我。(Tā cháng cháng yòng duǎn xìn lián xì wǒ.) - He often contacts me by text message.

**Usage Notes**:  
**短信** (duǎn xìn) specifically refers to SMS or text messages, not emails or instant messages. Commonly used as both noun and object of the verb **发** (fā, to send).

**Memory Aids**:  
Think of **短** (duǎn, short) + **信** (xìn, letter/message): a "short message" = text message!
