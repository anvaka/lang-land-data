# **鼠标 (shǔbiāo) - Mouse (computer)**

**Character Breakdown**:  
- **鼠** (shǔ) - "mouse/rat": Radical "鼠" (rat), overall shape resembles a rat with its tail.  
- **标** (biāo) - "mark/indicator": Left side is "木" (mù, wood), right top is "票" (piào, ticket), bottom stroke connects both-literally a "marker" on wood, extended to mean "indicator."

**Examples**:  
- 请把**鼠标**(shǔbiāo)递给我。 (Qǐng bǎ shǔbiāo dì gěi wǒ.) - Please pass me the mouse.  
- 你会用**鼠标**(shǔbiāo)双击吗？ (Nǐ huì yòng shǔbiāo shuāng jī ma?) - Can you double-click the mouse?  
- 我的**鼠标**(shǔbiāo)坏了。 (Wǒ de shǔbiāo huài le.) - My mouse is broken.

**Usage Notes**:  
- **鼠标**(shǔbiāo) refers specifically to a computer mouse, not a real animal.  
- Measure word: 个 (gè).

**Memory Aids**:  
- Imagine a **鼠**(shǔ, mouse) running across your desk, needing an "**indicator**" (**标**(biāo)) to show where it’s going - just like your computer mouse moves on the screen.
